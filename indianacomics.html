NIST is working to enhance how we measure greenhouse gas emissions in cities, with a particular focus on Indianapolis, Indiana. Their project, called the Indianapolis Flux Experiment (INFLUX), started back in 2010 and aims to create better tools for accurately assessing greenhouse gas data. Covering the period from January 1, 2011, to December 31, 2023, the research provides hourly measurements at specific locations. They track carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane (CH₄) levels, updating the information annually. The INFLUX team gathers a wide range of data, including continuous measurements from towers, airborne observations, and atmospheric profiling. This comprehensive approach helps reveal how greenhouse gas concentrations change over time and space, ultimately linking different methods of estimating emissions to provide a clearer picture of urban air quality.
